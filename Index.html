Methodi Ordinatio Ranking

This systematic literature review methodology¹ is based on three factors for selecting publications and InOrdinatio, which is an index to classify selected works by relevance. This index crosses the three main factors being evaluated in an article: impact factor, year of publication and number of citations. By applying the equation, researchers identify among the selected works the most relevant ones to compose their bibliographic portfolio.

The equation presents the weighting factor α, whose value to be attributed by the researchers might vary from 1 to 10. The closer the number is to one, the lower the importance the researcher will attribute to the criterion year, while the closer to 10, the higher is the importance of this criterion.
